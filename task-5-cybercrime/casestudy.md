**PHISHING**



Phishing is one of the most common cybercrimes today. It mainly takes advantage of human trust instead of relying on technical flaws. Phishing involves tricking people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details.



For example, a student might get a message saying their bank account will be blocked if they don’t verify it quickly. The message looks official, so they don’t think much and click the link. The link takes them to a website that looks exactly like the real bank website. Without realizing, the student enters their login details or OTP.



After that, the scammer gets access to their account. In some cases, money can even be transferred without the student knowing. By the time they realize something is wrong, it’s already too late.



Students are easy targets because we use phones and apps all the time and sometimes act quickly without checking properly. These scams mostly work because people panic or trust the message.



This shows that we should always be careful online. Even if something looks real, it’s better to double-check before clicking any link or sharing any details.
